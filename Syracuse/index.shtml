Syracuse Egg Coddlers

The Syracuse China Company, formerly known as the Onandaga Pottery Company, manufactured the ceramic body of egg coddlers for Premier Egg Cup, beginning in 1893. According to an 1893 flyer, the egg coddlers were finished with a "screwtop cover" made of solid German silver. Later versions of the coddler lids might have been made with another metal.

The coddlers are convex with or without a pedestal, with a very thin metal lid, and no lifting ring. There were three sizes of coddler body, intended to cook one, two or three eggs. The metal lids have some slight variation in depth. There were three backstamp styles, with colour differences, although they all contain the word SYRACUSE somewhere in the backstamp text.

Cataloguing of the variations of Syracuse egg coddler varieties has proven to be very difficult. There is the possibility that every Syracuse coddler with flowers is unique. If you have information about coddlers that are not listed in these pages, please do contact us.

There are five characteristics that we consider distinguishing of unique variants:

  • Pattern:
      The coddlers have a plain white glaze, or they are decorated with leaves and/or flowers. The leaf/flower patterns vary from coddler to coddler. They may have all been hand-applied as we have not seen two that are exactly alike. In some cases, the patterns seem faded or partially removed by wear and age ... so it is also a possibility that some coddlers that are reported as "plain white" had a pattern at some point in the past. We will attempt to uniquely label different patterns using our own "unknown pattern" nomenclature, since we have no knowledge of whether Syracuse ever assigned names to the patterns. A list of known patterns with thumbnail images follows this section.

  • Backstamp colour:
      Either orange, or green. We've recorded a "brown" ... although that may very well be a matter of colour nomenclature used by the individual.

  • Backstamp style:
      OPS - Consists of the words O.P. and SYRACUSE CHINA, without any framing or shaping. This backstamp was used by Syracuse from 1897 to 1920 on commercial ware and 1897 to 1926 on dinnerware. These coddlers might have been produced by Syracuse independently of the Premier Egg Cup contract.

      CIR - Consists of the words PREMIER EGG CUP CO. and PAT. JUN 13, 93. in a circle, and SYRACUSE, over N.Y. in the centre of the circle.

      EGG - Consists of a line drawing in the shape of an egg, with the words THE PREMIER EGG CUP CO. along the top, inside the egg-shaped line, SYRACUSE, N.Y. along the bottom, inside the line, and one, two, or three patent dates in the centre of the drawing. The words SYRACUSE CHINA. appear below the egg-shaped drawing in most cases.

    OPS backstamp CIR backstamp EGG backstamp

  • Coddler body style:
      Some coddlers have a pedestal, others do not.

    No Pedestal With Pedestal

  • Coddler size:
      We know of three sizes. The nomenclature is ours:
      S: Single size - 25/8 inches tall and 23/16 inches in diameter. For cooking one egg.
      D: Double size - 27/8 inches tall and 2½ inches in diameter. For cooking two eggs.
      XL: Extra Large size - 33/8 inches tall and 2¾ inches in diameter. For cooking three eggs.

    Single Double Extra Large
    Note: images are not at same relative scale

  • Number of patent dates in the backstamp:
      ZERO: No dates appear in the backstamp. OPS backstamps show no patent dates.
      ONE: One date: PAT JUN 13 - 93., appears in the backstamp. CIR backstamps show one date, as do some EGG backstamps have one date.
      TWO: Two dates: PAT JUN 13 - 93., and FEB 4 - 79, appear in the backstamp. Only EGG backstamps have two dates.
      THREE: Three dates: PAT JUN 13 - 93., FEB 4 - 79, and DEC 25 - 94, appear in the backstamp. Only EGG backstamps have three dates.
