Uncertain Manufacturer - UM11

These coddlers have the classic Blue Onion pattern. This pattern is sometimes referred to as the Blue Danube pattern. It is not certain that all variations described here were made by the same manufacturer.

These coddlers come in two shapes, convex with a pedestal, and concave with no pedestal.

The convex coddlers are known in a double size, with a square lifting ring. There is a blue line around the pedestal base. The following backstamp variations are known to exist for the convex variation:

  • Blue Onion in script with two crossed arrows above the text.
  • Blue Onion in script with two crossed swords and the letter A above the text.
  • The marks OL, Woods, Lucky in a circle, with Japan beneath.

The concave coddlers are known in a double size, with a square lifting ring. The following backstamp variations are known to exist for the convex variation:

  • No backstamp, sometimes with a MADE IN JAPAN sticker.
  • Blue Onion in script with two crossed arrows above the text.
  • Blue Onion in script with two crossed swords and the letter A above the text.
  • The marks OL, Woods, Lucky in a circle, with Japan beneath.

There is believed to be a single-sized coddler with this design that we have not yet verified. If you have a single-sized coddler with the Blue Onion pattern in your collection, please contact us.

There is a matching plate with the same design and backstamp that is often found with the coddler.
