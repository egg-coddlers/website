Max Rösler / Max Roesler Egg Coddlers

These coddlers are three inches tall, with the lid screwed on. The lid has a flat top and no lifting ring. The pattern on the sides and the lid include roosters, chicks, and hens.


Roosters, Hens, and Chickens - Example 1

This example has the following markings on the bottom:

  • THURINGIA marked in red
  • 4598/1 incised into the porcelain
  • 3260 in black ink
  • a blue shield with a rose insignia, with the letters RMR in mirror-image above the shield.

The seller on eBay had said that she found the company name Max Rösler (also written Roesler) in a book on German porcelain marks. That company had used the mark number 3260 and was in business from 1832 to 1872. However, that company was located in Rodach, which is not in Thuringia.


Roosters, Hens, and Chickens - Example 2

This example has the following markings on the bottom:

  • GERMANY marked in blue
  • RMR 4598/1 incised into the porcelain
  • 3094 in black ink
  • a blue shield with a rose insignia, with the letters RMR in mirror-image above the shield.

Another example is just like it except that the last characters in the incised marking number and the ink number are unreadable ... so it could be RMR 4598/? and 309? where the question marks are unknown characters.


Roosters, Hens, and Chickens - Example 3

This example has the following markings on the bottom:

  • GERMANY marked in blue
  • 4598/1 incised into the porcelain
  • a blue shield with an insignia (looks different from the rose shown above, with the letters RMR in mirror-image above the shield.

The seller of this coddler looked up the backstamp in a book of German porcelain marks, and these markings are associated with the porcelain manufacturer Max Rösler.

Plain White - Examples 5 and 6
