Wade Coddlers

Wade coddlers are made of pottery, including the lid. They are typically a dull brownish-bluish-green, and there is plenty of variation in colours found.


Sizes/Shapes

Wade coddlers come in a single and a double size.

The single size is just under 3 inches tall (measured to the top of the lifting ring), and 2 ½ inches in diameter at the mouth. The base is 1 11/16 inches in diameter.

The double size is 4 to 4 ¼ inches tall, and 2 ½ inches in diameter at the mouth, and the base is 1 11/16 inches in diameter.. The shape is cylindrical with a very slight taper, and a small pedestal.



Please note: the size/shape combinations that we have listed here are the ones that we have verified from existing collections. This is not an exhaustive list ... if you have a Wade coddler in your collection that does not exactly match any of the descriptions here, please do contact us and we'll update this information.


Patterns and Backstamps

Plain Ivory

This coddler has WADE and IRELAND embossed on the bottom. It is one of only two Wade coddlers that does not have the row of shamrocks around the top. To date, we've seen this in the double size only.


Plain Dark Brown Amber

This coddler has a row of shamrocks around the top, and BY WADE and IRELAND embossed on the bottom. We have only seen these in single size.
Single Size Dark Brown Amber - "By Wade Ireland" Backstamp

Dark Brown

This coddler does not have shamrocks around the top, but has chickens sitting on nests. The backstamp says BALLYFREE FARMS, WADE, IRISH PORCELAIN, and MADE IN IRELAND

We've seen this coddler in both the single and double size.

    These coddlers were produced in 1975 and were obtained with egg carton tokens. -- AC-16.

Glossy Brownish Bluish Green - A.S. Cooper Backstamp

This backstamp consists of the words MADE IN IRELAND and A.S. COOPER. There are several speculations about who A.S. Cooper was, or their relationship to Wade. According to a correspondant in Bermuda, we have learned the following:

    A.S Cooper & Sons is one of the Largest China Stores here in Bermuda. They have been established since the late 1800's. They brought in a lot of the Irish Wade products and I assume that they had them marked with there own store 'Logo' for publicity.

There are two body style variations of these coddlers. Some of these coddlers have the row of shamrocks completely encircling the body of the coddler. The backstamp lettering on these coddlers is flat. Other coddlers with this backstamp have the rows of shamrocks interrupted by two pairs of smooth vertical columns, similar to those found on the plain dark brown amber coddlers mentioned earlier. The coddlers with the vertical columns have a backstamp in relief (the letters extend out beyond the surface of the backstamp).
